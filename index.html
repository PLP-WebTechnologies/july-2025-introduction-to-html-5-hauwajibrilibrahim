Introduction

Nigeria is home to over 900 species of birds, making it one of the richest birding destinations in West Africa. These birds thrive in a range of ecosystems—from rainforests and savannahs to wetlands and urban gardens.

Notable Bird Species

African Grey Parrot

Known for its intelligence and ability to mimic speech, this parrot is native to the rainforests of southeastern Nigeria. Sadly, it faces threats from illegal wildlife trade.

Malachite Kingfisher

This small, brightly colored bird is often spotted near slow-moving rivers and lakes. Its shimmering blue feathers make it a favorite among bird watchers.

Pied Crow

Common in urban areas, the Pied Crow is recognizable by its white chest and loud, cawing call. It is adaptable and intelligent, often seen foraging in open fields and towns.

Bird Conservation in Nigeria

Birds face increasing threats due to deforestation, hunting, and climate change. Conservation efforts by local and international organizations aim to protect habitats and educate communities.

You can help by supporting eco-tourism, reporting wildlife trafficking, and planting native trees to create bird-friendly environments.
